7 Coach Summary

🎯 Primary Pattern Identified Good sequencing, decent speed, but a pattern of left‑biased pressure, steep hand path, and variable vertical timing. Power is more hands‑driven than rotation‑driven, with modest X‑Factor Stretch.

Key Opportunities:

  • Standardize tempo around 3.3–3.5 (eliminate the 2.4–2.8 "jab" swings).
  • Increase downswing X‑Factor Stretch slightly for more efficient speed.
  • Reduce Sway Gap IMP magnitude (less "crash left").
  • Shallow hand path (Hand Sway DAH and Hand Pull Down).
  • Stabilize vertical timing (Pelvis Lift Time into IMP).

Recommended Priorities (Ranked):

  1. Shallower hand path + less leftward crash (path & pressure).
  2. Tempo standardization (3.3–3.5) across all swings.
  3. Vertical timing drill for pelvis/chest (earlier, consistent push).
  4. Add a bit more rotational stretch in downswing.

8 Prescribed Drills & Next Steps

🥇 Drill 1: "Feet‑Together Shallow Path" Hit half‑wedges with feet together, feeling the club swing more around you, not down and left.
🥈 Drill 2: "Tempo 3‑Count" Count "1‑2‑3" to the top, "4" to impact; record swings and match your best‑contact tempos.
🥉 Drill 3: "Vertical Timing Pump" Rehearse 3 pumps where you stay in posture, then push up late (near impact), not early.

📅 Re‑test in: 10–14 days  |  Focus club: GW (then map to 8i and hybrid/long iron in later session)

Practice Constraints:

  • Short sessions (20–30 balls)
  • 2–3 focused drills per session
  • Always finish with 5 "gamified" shots to a target using your "new pattern"
