02 · The Problem

Three problems that quietly cost you deals

Sublime has rare capability — femtosecond precision, days-not-weeks turnaround, no MOQ. The problem isn't what you make. It's finding the ~5,000 companies worldwide building the devices that need your tubes, at the right moment.

The Invisible Prospect

Your best buyers are invisible to normal prospecting: a 12-person startup that just won a $2M NIH SBIR Phase II. A stealth spinout paying a KOL via CMS. A university lab that filed 3 patents but has no website yet.

Solution: track signals, not companies

The Timing Problem

Find them too early — they're not ready, you're noise. Too late (Series C, FDA cleared) — suppliers are locked in. The window is razor-thin: 12–18 months post-patent, with active funding and a trial starting.

Solution: a timing model that pinpoints the window

The Technical Fit Problem

Not every med-device maker needs tubular laser cutting. A generic filter yields 95% false positives — dental imaging, ophthalmics, HVAC "tubes." Outreach to the wrong fit wastes credibility.

Solution: an LLM reads every abstract & scores fit 0–100

What doesn't work for a niche like yours

📊 Buy a lead list

Generic, saturated, every competitor has it. Zero edge.

🔍 Google Ads / SEO

Buyers at this stage aren't searching "laser cut nitinol hypotube supplier." They don't know that's what they need yet.

🏢 Cold-call the big OEMs

Medtronic, Boston Scientific, J&J have entrenched supply chains and 18-month procurement cycles. Waste of time.

🎟️ Trade shows

Expensive, slow, and you mostly meet your competitors' existing customers.

03 · Architecture

From 46 data streams to a ranked daily feed

A six-stage pipeline ingests public and commercial data, resolves it into unified companies, qualifies it with an LLM, and scores every prospect on a 0–200 readiness index — updated nightly.

1

Ingestion

Scheduled extractors pull from REST APIs, HTML scrapers, and bulk downloads across 46 sources.

REST APIsHTML scrapingBulk CSV
2

Normalization

Company names canonicalized, addresses geocoded, date/HS/CPC/NAICS codes validated to a single schema.

3

Entity Resolution hardest part

"Acme Catheter Inc." in SBIR ≠ "ACME MEDICAL" in FDA. Fuzzy matching (Jaro-Winkler + Splink) fuses records into one canonical company, with a manual review queue for top prospects.

4

LLM Qualification

An LLM reads every grant, patent, and trial abstract and scores 0–100 for relevance to laser-cut medical tubes — eliminating aerospace/HVAC false positives automatically.

5

Scoring

A weighted, configurable model (0–200) overlays the buying-window timeline and assigns a band + recommended action.

6

Output

Dashboard feed, Slack/email alerts on threshold crossings, CRM sync, and CSV export — each lead ships with a "why this lead" evidence card.
