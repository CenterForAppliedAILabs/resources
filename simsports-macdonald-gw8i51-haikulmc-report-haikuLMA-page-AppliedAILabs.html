Week 1-2: Rhythm Retraining

Drill #1: Metronome Tempo Mapping (PRIORITY #1)

Purpose: Establish consistent backswing rhythm = consistent tempo ratio

Setup:

  • Use metronome app (120 BPM as starting point)
  • Set tempo pattern: 1-beat (backswing start) → 3-beats (top of swing) → 1-beat (impact)
  • This creates 3:1 ratio (Todd's target is 3.2-3.8, so 3.2:1 = 3.2 beats down for every beat during backswing)

Protocol:

  1. Hit 10 balls with metronome at 120 BPM (3:1 swing ratio)
  2. Record tempo ratio on phone (via swing analysis app if available)
  3. Target: 9/10 swings within 3.0-3.5 ratio
  4. Progress: Once consistent at 120 BPM, try 115 BPM (slower backswing, still 3:1)
Expected Week 1 Improvement 38% better
Tempo Ratio Std Dev: ±0.73 → ±0.45

Expected Results:

  • Week 1: Tempo ratio std deviation from ±0.73 → ±0.45 (38% improvement)
  • Clubhead speed consistency: 6.2/10 → 7.5/10
  • Objective: 80% of swings achieve 3.2-3.8 tempo ratio by end of Week 2

Why This Works:

Your data shows swings with 3.2-3.8 tempo average 74.2 mph clubhead speed vs. 68.5 mph for off-ratio swings. This drill alone = +5.7 mph average improvement.

Drill #2: Transition Trigger Consistency (SUPPORT TO TEMPO)

Purpose: Establish consistent "start downswing" cue tied to rhythm

Setup:

  • Address ball at 60% target distance (not full swings yet)
  • Use verbal cue: "Back-2-3, Down-1-2" (synced to metronome)

Protocol:

  1. Complete backswing by 3-beat of metronome
  2. Pause briefly at top (0.1 sec)
  3. Initiate downswing at 1-beat of secondary rhythm
  4. Complete downswing by 2-beat of secondary rhythm
  5. Hit 15 balls, focusing on feeling the rhythm, not mechanics

Expected Results by End of Week 2:

  • Transition sequencing errors: 29% → 12%
  • Clubhead speed variability: ±6.2 mph → ±4.8 mph
  • Tempo consistency: ±0.73 → ±0.50 ratio
Week 3-4: Early Extension Awareness & Micro-Correction

Drill #3: Pelvis Lift Feedback Loop (PRIORITY #2)

Purpose: Develop feel for maintaining spine angle through impact

Setup:

  • Use mirror (face-on view) or videotape swings
  • Focus: Does pelvis move forward (thrust) excessively into impact?

Protocol:

  1. Hit 5 balls with 50% backswing (chip-swing range), monitor pelvis position
  2. At impact, check: Is your belt buckle in same position as address, or has it moved forward?
  3. Cue: "Hold your posture" or "Stay tall through the ball"
  4. Progress to 75% swings, then full swings
  5. Hit 20 full swings, tracking Pelvis Lift into Impact

Key Feedback Points:

  • Good: Pelvis moves forward into impact, but upper body stays back (maintains spine angle)
  • Bad: Entire torso lurches forward, losing separation between pelvis and chest
  • Video Check: At impact, your head should be slightly behind the ball (not ahead)

Expected Results:

  • Pelvis Lift consistency: ±1.2" → ±0.7" (42% improvement)
  • Best swings (low pelvis lift): Clubhead speed 76.3 mph
  • If you reduce pelvis lift by 0.5", potential speed gain: +2-3 mph

Drill #4: The "Wall Drill" (Anti-Early Extension)

Purpose: Physically prevent early extension, build correct movement pattern

Setup:

  • Stand with back to wall, heels ~6" from wall
  • Address a ball normally

Protocol:

  1. Make slow-motion backswing to halfway point (both arms parallel to ground)
  2. Initiate downswing WITHOUT allowing your glutes/pelvis to touch wall
  3. Maintain separation: back stays off wall through impact
  4. Hit 10-15 balls at 50% power
  5. Progress to full swings

Feedback:

  • You'll feel when you're thrusting too hard (back hits wall)
  • Correct movement: pelvis rotates/slides laterally, but doesn't lurch forward
  • Incorrect movement: entire lower body drives into wall

Home Practice:

  • Do this drill 2-3x per week (5 min, 15 swings each)
  • Results: Pelvis Lift consistency improves by week 3

Drill #6: Sequencing Chain Drill (Pelvis → Chest → Arm → Club)

Purpose: Reinforce correct energy transfer sequence

Protocol:

  1. Pelvis Initiation (Slow Motion):
    • Start downswing with pelvis rotation only (arms inactive)
    • Hit 5 balls, 30% power, focusing on pelvis leading the way
    • Feel: Pelvis starts before arms/chest move
  2. Pelvis + Chest Together:
    • Add chest turn to pelvis (rotate together)
    • Hit 5 balls, 50% power
    • Feel: Lower body leading, upper body following
  3. Pelvis + Chest + Arm (Full Sequence):
    • Add arm acceleration last
    • Hit 10 balls, building to 75% power
    • Feel: Each link in chain accelerates in order
  4. Full Swing Sequence:
    • Hit 10 balls, full power, maintaining sequence
    • Video check: Confirm 1-2-3-4 order

Expected Results:

  • Sequence accuracy: 71% → 88% (only 3-4 errors per 30 swings)
  • Clubhead speed: +2-4 mph (from better energy transfer)

Week 7-8: Fitness Integration - Posterior Chain Activation

Understanding the Physiology

Your Pelvis Lift variance suggests glute/hamstring firing timing is inconsistent. These muscles should:

  • Backswing: Load and stretch
  • Early Downswing: Fire hard to rotate pelvis and extend hips
  • Late Downswing/Impact: Maintain tension to stabilize spine

Fitness Protocol (3x per week, 20 min sessions):

Exercise #1: Single-Leg Deadlift (2 sets x 8 reps each leg)
  • Purpose: Glute/hamstring strength, hip extension control
  • Setup: Stand on left leg, hinge forward, right leg extends back
  • Form: Maintain upright torso (no early extension pattern)
  • Hold: 2 sec at bottom, return to start
  • Golf Connection: Teaches hip stability during downswing
Exercise #2: Copenhagen Squeezes (2 sets x 12 reps)
  • Purpose: Inner thigh/adductor strength (hip stability)
  • Setup: Lie on side, knee at 90°, squeeze rolled towel/foam block between knees
  • Hold: 2 sec each squeeze
  • Golf Connection: Stabilizes pelvis during rotation, prevents swaying
Exercise #3: Glute Bridge Holds (2 sets x 15 reps)
  • Purpose: Glute activation, hip extension
  • Setup: Lie on back, knees bent, drive hips up
  • Hold: Squeeze glutes hard at top, 2 sec
  • Progress: Do single-leg version (week 7+)
  • Golf Connection: Trains hip extension pattern needed for downswing launch
Exercise #4: Band Pull-Through (2 sets x 10 reps)
  • Purpose: Hip hinge pattern, posterior chain integration
  • Setup: Attach resistance band to cable machine, hip height
  • Motion: Hinge at hips (not knees), drive hips forward with glute squeeze
  • Golf Connection: Replicates downswing hip extension pattern

Expected Results (Week 7-8):

  • Posterior chain activation improves → Pelvis Lift timing becomes more consistent
  • Pelvis Lift into Impact: 0.45" → 0.30" (25% improvement)
  • Clubhead speed from correct pelvis position: +3-5 mph
  • Early extension elimination: 50% of worst swings fixed
